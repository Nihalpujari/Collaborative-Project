**The Gourmet Pigeon**

In the heart of a bustling city square, where crumbs and laughter mingle on the pavement, a lone pigeon perches on the edge of a park bench—its feathers ruffled by the breeze, its beady eyes fixed with the intensity of a Michelin-starred chef. Before it lies a *tiny* slice of pizza, no bigger than a postage stamp, its golden crust glistening with a single drop of oil.

But this is no ordinary pigeon. This is *Chef Coobert*, a bird of refined tastes and impeccable table manners. With the precision of a surgeon, it grips a miniature fork (stolen from a nearby café’s outdoor seating) in its left claw, delicately spearing a single strand of melted mozzarella. The cheese stretches like a golden thread before snapping back into place. A satisfied *coo* escapes its beak.

A sparrow watches from a lamppost, beak agape. "Dude," it chirps, "you’re *supposed* to just peck at it." Chef Coobert side-eyes the sparrow with disdain, then daintily dabs the corner of its beak with a napkin (also stolen). "Amateur," it seems to say.

Nearby, a human child points and giggles. "Mom, the pigeon’s using a fork!" The mother, sipping her iced latte, nods sagely. "That’s why we don’t litter, sweetie. The birds are getting *too* civilized."

Chef Coobert ignores them all, savoring each bite with the gravitas of a food critic. The pepperoni is *slightly* over-salted, but the crust—ah, the crust is *perfection*. A crumb falls to the ground. A lesser pigeon would dive for it, but not Chef Coobert. It *never* double-dips.

As the last morsel disappears, the pigeon gives a satisfied sigh, tucks the fork into its wing (for later), and takes flight—leaving behind only a single, pristine napkin and the faintest whisper of garlic butter in the air.
