**Title: *The Dark Knight Peels***

The Batcave’s dim glow flickers over the stainless-steel counter, where a single, **gigantic onion** looms like a villain’s doomsday device. Its papery skin glistens under the harsh light, layers upon layers of potential tears waiting to be unleashed.

Batman stands over it, cape draped like a funeral shroud, his gloved hands gripping the knife with the same precision he uses to dismantle Gotham’s underworld. The blade flashes—*thunk*—a clean cut. The first slice releases a ghostly mist, curling into the air like the specter of his parents’ deaths.

His jaw tightens. The onion’s fumes creep into his cowl, stinging his eyes. A single tear—**no, not from grief, not from fear, but from the damn onion**—rolls down his chiseled cheek, glinting like a diamond in the shadows. Another follows. Then another.

Alfred’s voice crackles over the comms: *"Sir, are you… crying?"*
*"No,"* Batman growls, voice rough. *"It’s the onion."*
*"Of course, Master Wayne. Shall I fetch the goggles?"*
*"I don’t need goggles."* (He *always* needs goggles.)

The knife moves faster, a blur of vengeance against the vegetable. The onion falls apart, its layers surrendering like a defeated foe. Batman wipes his face with the back of his glove, leaving a smudge of mascara (because even the Bat can’t escape the *smoky eye* look).

Behind him, the Batcomputer hums, oblivious. Somewhere in Gotham, a criminal laughs. But here, in the heart of the cave, the world’s greatest detective is just a man… **brought low by produce.**
