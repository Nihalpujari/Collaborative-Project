**Scene Description – “Iron Man’s First Knit”**

*Setting:* A sleek, high‑tech workshop perched atop Stark Tower, its panoramic windows framing a snow‑blanketed cityscape. Inside, the usually bustling lab is hushed, except for the soft *click‑clack* of a knitting needle meeting yarn and the occasional whirr of Tony’s repulsor cooling system.

*The Suit:* Tony Stark’s iconic red‑gold armor is still on—though a few panels have been swapped for lighter, flexible composites to give his arms more freedom of movement. The gauntlet on his right arm now sports a *custom‑made, detachable module* that gently holds a pair of knitting needles. The left glove, equipped with an array of micro‑servos, delicately twirls a skein of yarn like a conductor’s baton.

*The Yarn:* A massive ball of ultra‑soft, melt‑resistant filament sits on a magnetic turntable. It’s a kaleidoscope of colors—crimson, sapphire, emerald, sunrise orange, and icy teal—each strand shimmering with a faint, metallic sheen. The yarn is engineered to stay warm on the wearer’s neck, but still soft enough to be knotted by a pair of mechanical fingers.
