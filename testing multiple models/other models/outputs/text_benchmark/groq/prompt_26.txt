The mummy, wrapped from head to toe in tattered, yellowed bandages, shuffled towards the revolving door, its movements stiff and awkward. As it pushed against the door, it began to turn, but the mummy's momentum was slow, and it became wedged in the small space.

The door creaked and groaned as the mummy tried to free itself, but its bandages kept getting caught on the metal frames and handles. The more it struggled, the more entangled it became. The mummy's wrappings began to unravel, unwinding like a spool of thread as it spun around in circles, stuck in the door.

As people passed by, they couldn't help but stare at the bizarre sight. Some giggled, while others looked on in confusion. One person attempted to assist the mummy, but it was too stuck, and the door was too jammed.

The mummy's bandages continued to unwind, revealing glimpses of dried, parchment-like skin underneath. The door creaked and groaned, threatening to collapse under the strain. Finally, with one last, mighty push, the mummy managed to extricate itself, stumbling out into the open air, its bandages in disarray.

As it stood up, dazed and confused, the mummy looked down at its unwrapped state and let out a mighty sigh. It began to rewrap its bandages, muttering to itself in a long-forgotten language. The people around it couldn't help but laugh at the absurdity of the situation, and the mummy, despite its frustration, couldn't help but join in, its dry, papery laughter echoing through the air.
