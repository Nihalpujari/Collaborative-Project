(Sir Reginald, a medieval knight, stands in the middle of a bustling city, dressed in his full armor, holding a modern smartphone. He looks at it with a mixture of confusion and fascination.)

Sir Reginald: (to himself) What sorcery is this? A small glass window that showeth images and words, yet doth not seem to be magical.

(He pokes at the screen with his finger, trying to get it to respond.)

Sir Reginald: (muttering) How doth one make it work? Doth one need to whisper incantations or make a sacred gesture?

(A passerby, a young woman, stops and looks at Sir Reginald with amusement.)

Woman: (laughing) Oh my god, are you okay? Do you need some help with that?

Sir Reginald: (startled) Ah, good morrow fair maiden! I am Sir Reginald, a knight of the realm. And thou, kind stranger, dost thou know the secrets of this mystical device?

Woman: (still chuckling) Uh, yeah. It's a smartphone. You just need to swipe your finger across the screen to unlock it.

Sir Reginald: (excitedly) Swipe? Thou meanest, like a sword stroke?

Woman: (laughing) Kind of, but not exactly. Let me show you.

(She takes the phone from Sir Reginald and demonstrates how to swipe the screen to unlock it.)

Sir Reginald: (amazed) Marvelous! The glass window hath come to life!

(He starts to navigate through the phone's menu, touching various icons and buttons.)

Sir Reginald: (excitedly) What wonders lie within this device? A map of the land? A book of knowledge? A messenger to send word to my fellow knights?

Woman: (smiling) Yeah, it's all of those things and more. You can access the internet, send emails, take pictures...

Sir Reginald: (interrupting) Pictures? Thou meanest, like a portrait of a fair maiden?

Woman: (laughing) Exactly! You can take pictures with the phone's camera.

(Sir Reginald starts to take pictures of the surrounding area, marveling at the phone's ability to capture images.)
