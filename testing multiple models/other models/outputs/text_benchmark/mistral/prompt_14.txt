**Title: "Rockin’ the Ripples"**

A tiny, lime-green tree frog with golden eyes perches on a lily pad the size of a dinner plate, its webbed toes curled around the edge like a seasoned performer gripping a mic stand. The frog’s slender fingers—each tipped with tiny suction cups—strum a **miniature electric guitar** the size of a harmonica, its neon-blue body covered in tiny stickers (a skull, a lightning bolt, and a tiny "1" for its first gig). The guitar’s **amp**, a hollowed-out acorn with a spiderweb-thin speaker, sits on a nearby pad, humming with feedback as the frog shreds a solo, its tongue flicking in rhythm like a metronome.

The lily pad stage tilts slightly under the weight of the performance, sending **ripples** through the water like a crowd moshing. Fireflies (the frog’s roadies) hover in a halo around its head, their glow pulsing like stage lights. A **dragonfly** in the front row claps its wings in applause, while a **sleepy turtle** in the back nods along, one eye cracked open. Even the **moon**, reflected in the pond, seems to lean in closer, as if the whole universe is vibing to the frog’s **punk-rock croaks**.

**Bonus details (if you want to go wild):**
- The guitar’s **strap** is a dandelion stem, frayed at the edges.
- A **tiny setlist** is written on a leaf in dew: *"1. Tadpole Blues 2. Fly Me to the Moon (but like, *angry*) 3. Encore: Ribbit Riot"*.
- A **beetle** in the audience holds up a lighter (a matchstick) during the ballad.
- The frog’s **pick** is a fish scale, glinting under the bioluminescent glow of algae.
