**The Avocado Drummer**

In the heart of a sunlit kitchen, perched atop a wooden cutting board like a tiny stage, sits an avocado—plump, glossy, and *very* serious about its craft. Its pit, now hollowed out and repurposed as a snare drum, sits between its two halves, which have been carefully carved into drumsticks. The left half taps out a crisp *rat-a-tat-tat* on the pit’s edge, while the right half swings down with a muffled *thump* on a walnut shell (the bass drum, obviously).

A slice of lime, balanced precariously on the counter, serves as a cymbal—*clang!*—whenever the avocado leans just right. The rhythm is chaotic but oddly hypnotic: a mix of jazz, salsa, and whatever genre "fruit percussion" falls under. Nearby, a grape rolls its eyes (metaphorically) and mutters, *"Show-off."*

The avocado, oblivious to the judgment, grins with its seed-shaped smile and keeps playing—because when life gives you avocados, you *make guacamole… or a one-fruit band.*
