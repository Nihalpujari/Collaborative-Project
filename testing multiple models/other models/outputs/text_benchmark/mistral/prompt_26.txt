**Title: *The Mummy’s Spin Cycle***

The revolving door at the grand, ancient museum spun with a rhythmic *whoosh-whoosh-whoosh*, its glass panels reflecting the flickering torchlight of the exhibit hall. Inside, the newest attraction—*"Imhotep’s Curse: A Mummy’s Revenge"*—had just opened, and the crowds were thick.

Then, disaster struck.

A particularly *enthusiastic* mummy, freshly unwrapped (or at least *mostly* unwrapped) by overzealous archaeologists, had decided to stretch its bandaged legs. With a dramatic flourish of linen, it lurched toward the exit—only to misjudge the timing.

**THUNK.**

One bandaged arm shot out to brace itself. The door kept spinning. The mummy’s other arm flailed. The door *did not care*.

**"AAAAAAH—"** (or at least, the mummy’s version of a scream, which sounded like a vacuum cleaner eating a sheet) **"—RRRRRGGH—"**

Now, the mummy was *part* of the door. Its torso was wedged in one panel, its legs kicking wildly in the next, while its head—still wrapped in tattered gauze—bobbed helplessly as the door rotated. Tourists on the other side screamed, dropped their souvenir hieroglyphic mugs, and whipped out their phones.

**"IS THIS PART OF THE EXHIBIT?!"** a kid yelled, pointing.

**"NO, TIMMY, THAT’S JUST—"** His dad paused. **"…Actually, I don’t know."**

The mummy’s bandages began to unravel further, trailing behind it like a ghostly scarf. One particularly long strip snagged on the door’s hinge, yanking the mummy into a full-body *twist*. Now it was spinning *faster*, a whirlwind of ancient linen and curses.
