**Scene Description – “Mummy in the Revolving Door”**

*Setting*: A sleek, glass‑fronted shopping mall. Sunlight streams through the skylight, casting glittering reflections on the polished marble floor. A large, cylindrical revolving door—its glass panels gleaming like frozen waterfalls—whirs gently in the bustle of shoppers.

*The Mummy*: A towering figure draped in ancient Egyptian bandages, each linen strip stained with centuries‑old dust and a faint, sepia‑tone patina. The bandages are meticulously wrapped, overlapping like the folds of a weathered map, with a faint outline of a once‑vibrant Ankh pendant peeking through the fabric near the chest. Its hollow eyes, empty of any modern technology, stare blankly forward, while a faded, regal headdress—once a golden cobra crest—now sits crookedly atop the head, its metal glint dulled by age.

*The Incident*: The mummy steps—or rather, lumbers—into the revolving door, its sand‑filled feet making soft thuds against the floor. The door’s mechanism, designed for swift human traffic, catches on the thick layers of linen. As the panels turn, a few of the bandages snag on the rotating gear, causing a tense tug‑and‑pull. The door’s smooth motion slows to a creak, then halts altogether, trapping the ancient relic mid‑rotation.
