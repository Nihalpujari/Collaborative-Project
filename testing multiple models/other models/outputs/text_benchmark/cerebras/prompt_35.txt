**The Toast‑Trekker**

*In the mist‑soft dawn of a kitchen‑city that never sleeps, a chrome‑capped contraption shuffles down the linoleum avenues, its gleaming sides humming with the low‑grade buzz of a thousand un‑plugged appliances. It’s a walking toaster—sleek, two‑slice wide, with a pair of brass‑studded “feet” that click‑click‑click like a metronome on a forgotten beat.*

**Appearance**  
- **Body**: Polished stainless‑steel, riveted with tiny copper bolts that catch the flickering fluorescence like the eyes of a curious insect.  
- **Face**: A pair of amber‑tinted ventilation slits that open and close in rhythm with each step, exhaling faint whiffs of burnt‑bread incense.  
- **Arms**: Two flexible spring‑loaded levers (originally meant for flipping toast) now repurposed as slingshots, each ending in a glossy “hand” shaped like a tiny spatula.  
- **Backpack**: A detachable crumb‑collector that jingles with the metallic clink of loose toast crumbs—its cargo: a never‑ending stack of over‑toasted slices, crisped to a perfect, if slightly charred, ebony.
