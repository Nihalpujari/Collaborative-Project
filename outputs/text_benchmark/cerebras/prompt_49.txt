### 🎉 The Scene in Words

On a bright, sun‑splashed afternoon in the prehistoric playground, a **bushy‑scaled Triceratops** has decided it’s time to try something new. Instead of the usual leaf‑chewing and thunder‑foot stomps, it hops onto a **gleaming, chrome‑finished pogo stick** that glints like a comet in the sky.

The stick is a marvel of Jurassic engineering: a long, sturdy shaft of **fiberglass‑reinforced amber**, capped with a **rubber‑plated foot‑pad** that squeaks a friendly “boing!” each time it lands on the soft loam. The Triceratops—its three horns gleaming with pride—grips the curved handle with its front claws, eyes narrowing in concentration.

With a mighty *thump* of its massive hind legs, the dinosaur launches upward. The pogo stick compresses, then springs back, sending the creature soaring a foot or two above the towering ferns. The ground below ripples with each bounce, and a chorus of giggling **pterodactyls** swoops overhead, cheering “**Whoo‑hoo!**” as the dinosaur performs a perfect series of hops.
