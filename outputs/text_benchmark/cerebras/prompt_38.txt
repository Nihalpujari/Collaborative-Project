**The Great Roll‑Out**

The morning sun had just begun to sputter over the skyline, turning the glass facades of downtown into a glittering field of mirrored shards. Out of a tiny, unremarkable office kitchenette—where a coffee machine drummed out its usual lullaby and a lone plant drooped in protest—an innocent roll of white, over‑padded toilet paper slipped free from its cardboard cradle.

It was a simple thing: two layers of fluffy tissue, a perforated spine, a glossy cardboard belly. Yet, as the last hand‑tied parcel of its packaging gave way, the roll found itself perched on the edge of the recycling bin, teetering like a newborn on a cliff. A sudden gust, a sigh from the building’s ventilation shaft, nudged it forward. Gravity answered with a soft, conspiratorial sigh.

*Whoosh!* The roll began its daring descent onto the polished marble lobby. It bounced once—light as a feather—against a marble column, sending a faint shiver through the marble that was barely enough to catch the attention of a nearby janitor, who glanced up from his mop, frowned, and went back to his work.

From the lobby, the roll rolled onto the glossy tile of the main atrium. Here, the world was louder: the chatter of commuters, the clack of high heels, the metallic hum of elevators. A sleek espresso machine hissed, a pair of businesspeople argued about quarterly earnings, and a street‑performer on a corner strummed a melancholy jazz riff on his saxophone.
