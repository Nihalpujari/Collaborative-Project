**The Veggie‑Riff: A Broc‑Headbanging Rock Show**

*Venue*  
Imagine the underground cellar of a bustling farmer’s market, its brick walls plastered with reclaimed lettuce leaves and old grape‑vine drapes. A flickering neon sign shaped like a crown of florets glows “**B.R.O.C. – Broccoli Rock Out Club**”. The stage is a reclaimed wooden pallet, lit from below by a cascade of soft, green‑tinted LEDs that pulse in time with the music.

*The Band*  
- **Lead Singer “Stalk‑Star”** – a towering stalk of broccoli, its crown a thicket of emerald florets that sway like a wild mane. A tiny mic‑stand—actually a slender carrot—holds a retro‑styled microphone shaped like a beet root. When he sings, his voice crackles with the gritty growl of a vintage tube amp, resonating deep in the ribcage of every veggie in the audience.

- **Guitarist “Riff‑Root”** – a lean, wiry broccoli spear wielding a custom‑made electric guitar carved from a polished pumpkin shell, its strings glinting with dew‑kissed cucumber fibers. Each chord sends a cascade of leafy sparks, while his solo riffs bend the very air, causing nearby parsley leaves to rustle in admiration.

- **Bassist “Thick‑Stem”** – a stout broccoli head slung low, its bass line thudding like a distant thunderstorm across the field of radish drums. The instrument is a bass made from an old onion husk, its resonant body humming with low frequencies that make the ground vibrate—so much so that a few nearby carrots hop in rhythm.
