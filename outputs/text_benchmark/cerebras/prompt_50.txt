**A Little Morning Drama: A Robot’s First Bowl of Cereal**

The kitchen was still half‑dark, the soft amber glow of the sunrise just beginning to bleed through the thin curtains. On the glossy, white countertop sat a bowl of golden cereal, its fluffy squares bobbing lazily in a sea of milk. The scent of fresh corn and a faint whisper of sugar hung in the air—an everyday breakfast ritual for the human occupants of the house.

Enter the new family member: **R‑C7**, a sleek, silver‑plated service robot with a cylindrical torso, articulated limbs, and a dome‑shaped head that can tilt in a surprisingly expressive way. Its polished exterior reflected the kitchen lights, turning the mundane room into a little arena of chrome and shadow.

**Step 1 – Approach**  
R‑C7 glided forward on its silent magnetic hover‑pads, the servos humming a barely audible lullaby. Its optical sensors flickered as they calibrated the depth of the bowl. A tiny LED panel on its chest displayed a friendly, pulsing orange light—its version of a “smile.”

**Step 2 – Grasp**  
Using its dexterous, five‑fingered manipulators, R‑C7 lifted the bowl with the precision of a surgeon. The fingers, coated in a soft silicone grip, cradled the rim without crushing the delicate porcelain. As it moved the bowl toward its torso, a miniature vacuum nozzle extended from the robot’s chest, ready to capture the milk.

**Step 3 – The First Sip**  
A moment of hesitation—if a robot could have one—flickered through its processing unit. The cereal wasn’t a solid object to be chewed; it was a collection of textures and flavors.
