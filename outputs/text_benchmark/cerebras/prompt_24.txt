**Superman’s Wax‑i‑ng Trouble**

The bright lights of Metropolis glittered as Superman glided down the city’s main boulevard, his cape fluttering behind him like a billboard for hope. Today, however, his mission was decidedly more… domestic.

On a whim (and after a particularly stubborn piece of hair stuck to his front knee during a rescue, much to his chagrin), Clark Kent had booked an appointment at “Shear Bliss,” the newest boutique salon on the block. The idea of a quick, painless leg wax seemed simple enough—after all, no one could possibly stand the heat of a Kryptonian’s laser stare without melting the wax, right?

The salon itself was a haven of soft music, scented candles, and pastel décor. A tiny bell jingled as Superman stepped through the door, his presence causing a few heads to turn, though most patrons pretended not to notice. He gave a friendly nod to the receptionist, who practically squealed, “Welcome! You must be… Sir, are you…?”

“Just a regular guy,” Clark replied with a smile, slipping into a stylish robe that, while not exactly his usual, still fit snugly thanks to a bit of superhero tailoring.
