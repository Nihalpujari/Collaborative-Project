**Title: “Chrono‑Strider” – The Grandfather Clock on Robotic Legs**

---

### 1. Visual Snapshot

Imagine a towering, mahogany‑finished grandfather clock, its polished veneer catching the soft glow of a dimly lit workshop. The clock’s traditional brass pendulum and clock face are still there, but the entire case is now perched atop a pair of sleek, cyber‑metal legs that end in articulated, rubber‑capped feet.

- **Upper Body (the Clock)**  
  - **Case:** Deep walnut wood, with intricate marquetry patterns forming spirals of time‑tide motifs. The brass panels are polished to a warm, amber sheen.  
  - **Clock Face:** A classic Roman numeral dial, but now overlaid with a faint, holographic overlay that subtly shifts to show the current date and a low‑pulse heartbeat of the machine. Around the edge, a thin ring of LEDs pulses gently, syncing with the tick‑tock.  
  - **Pendulum:** A suspended, chrome‑coated double‑pendulum that swings in perfect rhythm, serving as the clock’s “heart” and a kinetic stabilizer for the robot’s balance.  
  - **Hands:** Traditional copper hour and minute hands, each ending in tiny, gear‑shaped tips that click with each movement. The second hand is a luminous fiber‑optic filament that spins in a soft blue glow.

- **Robotic Legs**
