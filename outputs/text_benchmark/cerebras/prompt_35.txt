**The Toast‑Bouncer**

*Brief visual guide for illustrators*  
- **Body:** A chrome‑polished toaster about the size of a small suitcase. Its side panels are embossed with a jaunty smiley‑face grill, and a pair of brass “feet” (actually its two heating coils) click and whir when it walks.  
- **Head:** A rounded, slightly dented lid that opens like a tiny garage door, revealing the warm, amber‑glow interior. A pair of oversized “ears”—tiny copper antennae—wiggle with each step.  
- **Arms:** Two stubby, spring‑loaded levers extending from the sides, ending in mechanical claws that can pinch and fling.  
- **Weapon:** Slices of dark‑brown, almost‑charred toast—perfectly crisp, a little jagged at the edges—are tossed with a rubbery “pop!” The toast arcs through the air, spins like a boomerang, and returns to the toaster’s waiting claws with a soft “crunch.”
