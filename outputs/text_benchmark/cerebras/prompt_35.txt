**Scene Setting**

Imagine a bustling downtown street at the crack of morning—cobblestones still slick with a faint drizzle, the smell of fresh coffee hanging in the air, and the low hum of early commuters. Amidst the rhythm of city life, a most unlikely sight waddles forward: a gleaming, chrome‑finished toaster on tiny, rubber‑capped wheels, its side panels polished to a mirror‑like shine. Its brand‑new “Walk‑Assist” firmware flickers a cheerful amber glow across its LED display, spelling out “GOOD MORNING!” in looping cursive.

- **Body**: The toaster’s body is rectangular, but with rounded edges that suggest friendliness. Its front face houses the usual pair of bread slots, now slightly ajar to accommodate a built‑in conveyor belt that feeds the toast out the top.
- **Wheels**: Two sturdy, pneumatic wheels rotate beneath a low‑profile chassis, giving the machine a smooth, rolling gait that mimics a gentle stroll.
- **Arms**: Extending from either side are sleek, stainless‑steel “toasting arms” – flexible, articulated appendages ending in small, spring‑loaded catapults. These are the toaster’s “arms” for the upcoming boomerang display.
