**A vivid snapshot in words**

Imagine a still pond at the golden hour of a summer evening. The water’s surface is a glassy mirror, rippled only by the occasional sigh of a dragonfly. In the very centre, a broad, emerald‑green lily pad floats like a natural stage, its veins of deep green radiating outward like the ribs of a harp.

Perched on the pad is a plump, bright‑green frog—its skin shimmering with the faintest hint of amber in the waning light. Its eyes, the glossy, bulbous kind that seem to hold entire worlds in them, are half‑closed in concentration, a tiny smudge of water‑kissed purple across the iris. Around its throat hangs a tiny black leather strap, just big enough to hold a miniature electric guitar.

The guitar itself is a marvel of frog‑scale engineering:

- **Body:** A polished, walnut‑brown slab the size of a palm, with a sleek contour that mirrors the shape of a real electric guitar but scaled down to fit comfortably on the lily pad. A single, silver‑sparkling pickguard gleams in the sun.
- **Strings:** Four thin, silvery threads stretched taut across a tiny, polished bridge. They vibrate with each delicate pluck, sending a faint, buzzing resonance across the pond.
