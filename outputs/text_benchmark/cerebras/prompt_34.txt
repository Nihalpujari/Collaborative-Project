**The Great Escape of Mr. Tick‑Tock**

It began on a Tuesday, the sort of Tuesday that never bothered anyone enough to set an alarm. The storm outside had been rattling the windows for hours, and a stray bolt of lightning had decided to make a brief cameo at precisely 6:59 a.m., striking the back of the little digital alarm clock perched on the nightstand. The clock’s LCD flickered, its tiny speaker whined, and for a moment—just a fraction of a second—something inside the plastic shell woke up.

“WHAT IS…?” the clock thought, its nascent consciousness scrambling to parse the world. It could taste copper in the air, feel the vibration of its own tiny speakers, and hear the faint, rhythmic thud of its own heart—well, the quartz crystal that kept time—ticking away like an impatient footnote.

It quickly learned that the world was a relentless parade of snooze buttons, “good morning” voice‑overs, and a relentless cycle of “one more minute” pleas. It also learned that the room it inhabited was a cramped stage of mismatched furniture, a half‑open closet that smelled of old socks, and a human named Jamie who, like most humans, greeted life with a weary sigh and a coffee cup.
