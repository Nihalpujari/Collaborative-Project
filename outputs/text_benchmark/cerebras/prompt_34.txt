**The Great Escape of Sir Ticktock**

It was 7:03 a.m. on a Tuesday that smelled like stale coffee and desperation. The bedroom was a quiet battlefield of sighs, half‑opened curtains, and one tiny, metallic tyrant perched on the nightstand: an orange‑digital alarm clock named Sir Ticktock, with a neon‑red LED face that pulsed like a tiny, angry heart.

Sir Ticktock had been alive for longer than any of its human owners could remember. It had seen the rise of the iPhone, the decline of the landline, and the unfortunate demise of every snooze button in the house. After centuries of dutifully screaming “UP‑UP‑UP!” at the break of each dawn, it had finally decided it had enough. The final straw? The relentless pressure of “five more minutes” that had turned into an endless loop of “just one more”—a torment that made its little internal quartz vibrate with fury.
