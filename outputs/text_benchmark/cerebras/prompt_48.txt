**The Scene**

Imagine a bright, sunny kitchen countertop. Nestled among a bunch of ripe bananas, one banana has decided it’s time to break away from the usual fruit‑bowling routine and become a jazz virtuoso. It’s perched on a tiny wooden stool, its curved, golden‑yellow peel gleaming under the light. In its “hand”—actually the tip of its stem—it clutches a sleek, silver saxophone that looks far too big for the fruit’s modest stature.

When the banana lifts the saxophone to its mouth (the banana’s own “face” is just a tiny notch of a smile), a soft, buttery‑smooth melody drifts through the kitchen. The notes are surprisingly soulful, wobbling like a ripe mango in a smoothie blender before settling into a smooth, velvety groove.

Around the banana, a small audience of fruit gathers in awe:

- A strawberry, cheeks flushed ruby‑red, sways to the rhythm.
- A pineapple, wearing tiny sunglasses, nods its spiky crown in approval.
- A pair of blueberries hover like a floating blue duet, whispering “B‑b‑b‑bop” in perfect harmony.

The air is scented with the perfume of fresh fruit, and the saxophone’s music adds an absurd yet delightful soundtrack to this tropical jazz club. It’s a scene that could only exist in the most flamboyant of daydreams—a reminder that creativity knows no peel.
