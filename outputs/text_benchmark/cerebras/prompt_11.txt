**Picture This: A Penguin’s Hula‑Hoorah**

- **The Star:** A plump, tuxedo‑clad little emperor penguin stands proudly on a sun‑kissed, powder‑white beach. Its sleek black‑and‑white feathers are crisp, as if freshly waxed for a formal gala—yet today, the penguin is trading its stern, Antarctic demeanor for a splash of tropical flair.

- **The Skirt:** Wrapped snugly around its middle is a bright, hand‑woven grass skirt that shimmers in shades of emerald and amber. The strands of grass sway rhythmically with every step, catching the ocean breeze and adding a soft rustle that rivals the gentle lapping of waves.

- **The Pose:** With one flipper lifted high and the other resting lightly on its belly, the penguin arches its back in a graceful “hale‑‘aupuni” (the Hawaiian word for hula). Its head is slightly tilted, eyes twinkling with mischievous delight, and a tiny, contented smile curls at the edge of its beak.

- **The Setting:** Behind the penguin, a crystal‑clear lagoon gently laps at the shoreline, reflecting a sky painted in pastel pinks, oranges, and purples as the sun sets. A few palm fronds sway lazily in the distance, and a chorus of tropical birds—perhaps a flamboyant flamingo and a cheeky toucan—watch the performance with curious bobbing heads.
