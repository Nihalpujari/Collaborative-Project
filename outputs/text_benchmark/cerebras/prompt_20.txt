**Scene: “The Green Swan” – A Ballet Premiere Starring Shrek**

*Stage Layout*  
The theater’s deep‑maroon velvet curtains are pulled back to reveal a polished black‑tiled proscenium stage. A single, warm spotlight hangs from the ceiling, casting a soft halo of amber light that falls like a gentle sunrise on the center of the floor. Behind Shrek, a faint silhouette of a moonlit swamp can be seen in the backdrop, rendered in watercolor‑soft pastel tones—a nod to his humble origins.

*The Performer*  
Shrek stands tall at the center of the stage, his massive, emerald‑green frame a striking contrast against the sleek black tiles. He’s garbed in a delicate, diaphanous ballet costume that clings to his limbs like spun silk, fashioned from pale ivory chiffon that ripples with each breath. The costume is tailored to accommodate his broad shoulders, generous torso, and round belly, yet it’s surprisingly fluid—its layers of tulle and satin fluttering about his hips with an ethereal lightness. A tiny, silver tiara rests precariously atop his shaggy, mushroom‑capped hair, catching the spotlight and scattering tiny specks of light across the audience.

*The Pose*  
Shrek is captured mid‑arabesque, one leg extended backward in a perfect, feather‑light line, while his opposite arm is lifted high, fingers gently curved, mirroring the elegance of a swan’s neck. Despite his hulking size, his posture is poised: his core is engaged, his shoulders relaxed, and his eyes—normally twinkling with mischief—are now focused, serene, and slightly wistful. The weight of his body is distributed so perfectly that his raised foot appears to barely touch the floor, as if he’s hovering on a cushion of air.
