**Title: “Moon‑Bagpipe Serenade”**

*Scene Overview*  
Under a black, star‑splattered sky, a solitary astronaut stands on the powder‑fine regolith of the Sea of Tranquility. The horizon is a perfect, stark curve where the gray‑white lunar surface meets the infinite blackness of space. Far off, a thin crescent of Earth hangs like a blue‑green marble, its cloud‑swirled continents faintly illuminated by the Sun’s harsh glare.

*The Astronaut*  
The astronaut’s suit is the next‑generation Exploration EVA (Extravehicular Activity) rig—sleek, white‑silver composite plates with a faint metallic sheen, punctuated by dark, heat‑reflective ribbons that run along the torso and limbs. The helmet’s visor, a faceted sapphire, reflects the distant Earth and the glint of the bagpipes’ polished wood. A soft, amber glow emanates from the suit’s life‑support system, casting a gentle halo around the figure.

*The Bagpipes*  
Balanced on the astronaut’s chest, the bagpipes are an anachronistic yet striking juxtaposition. The chanter is made of a dark, lacquered walnut, its finger holes inlaid with tiny brass studs that gleam under the Sun’s glare. The drones, slender and silver‑toned, extend outward like metallic reeds, each tuned to a mournful harmonic that would echo across the vacuum—if sound could travel. The bag itself, a deep, burnished leather, is strapped tightly around the astronaut’s waist, its seams reinforced with silver stitching that catches the faint light.
