Laid there beside a goblet of ale and a scroll of vellum is an object unlike any he has ever seen—a sleek, glass rectangle that glows with an inner light that shifts through the colors of sunrise and dusk.

A strange voice, melodious and polite, erupts from the device: “Hello, Sir Aldric. How may I assist you today?” He gasps, the words reverberating off the stone walls. For a heartbeat he thinks the enchantments of a wizard might be at work. He steadies his breath, clears his throat, and, still half‑covered in chainmail, tentatively slides his finger across the glass, watching icons glide like tiny heraldic shields.
