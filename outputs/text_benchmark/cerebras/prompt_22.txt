**Sir Percival of Eastbrook and the “Mystical Glass‑Stone”**

The rain hammered the thatch of the tavern’s roof like a thousand tiny arrows, and the hearth’s fire sputtered in protest. Sir Percival, still bearing the dented helm and battered chainmail of the last battle, had been granted an unexpected boon by the village wizard: a small, smooth slab of glass that glowed with a light of its own.

“It is a gift of the Lady of Light,” the wizard had whispered, pressing the slab into Percival’s gauntleted hand. “In times of danger, it will guide you, speak to you, and even summon the wind itself!” The wizard’s eyes twinkled, though Percival suspected the wizard’s real motive was simply to see if the knight could be persuaded to trade his horse for a better marketable novelty.
