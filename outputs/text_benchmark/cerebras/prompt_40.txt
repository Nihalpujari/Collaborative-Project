**A Cloud in the Sky Blowing Bubble‑Gum**

*Imagine the lazy, cotton‑soft world of a summer sky. A lone cumulus, plump and pillowy, drifts lazily over a sun‑kissed meadow. But this isn’t any ordinary cloud—its bright, vapor‑white body is tinged with a faint rose‑blush, as if it’s been dusted with powdered sugar and a secret hint of candy.*

From a tiny, invisible mouth tucked somewhere on its lower edge, the cloud exhales a slow, steady stream of sticky, pink bubble‑gum vapor. The gum glistens like sunrise on a glass marble, catching the sunlight and turning every gust into a shimmering ribbon of pastel pink. As the wind catches it, the bubble‑gum curls into playful loops: first a small, perfect sphere that hangs suspended, wobbling like a jelly‑bean on a string; then a larger, mischievous spiral that stretches and snaps back, scattering delicate, pearly droplets of sweet‑scented mist over the fields below.

Below, the world reacts. Children pause mid‑run, eyes wide, pointing up at the airborne confection. A farmer’s cows, usually unbothered by the sky, turn their heads, nostrils flaring as a faint vanilla‑cinnamon aroma drifts down.
