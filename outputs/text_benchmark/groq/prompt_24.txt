The Man of Steel sat uncomfortably in the salon chair, his iconic red and blue suit awkwardly bunched up around his waist. The beautician, a petite woman with a kind smile, stood before him, wielding a waxing strip like a tiny flag.

"Okay, Superman, this might sting a little," she warned, trying to hide her own nervousness.

Superman, the being with skin as tough as steel and eyes that could see through walls, nodded bravely. He had always been invulnerable to pain, but he knew that his secret identity as Clark Kent required him to blend in with humanity. And what was more human than a little discomfort during a leg wax?

The beautician applied the wax to Superman's leg, spreading it evenly across the hairs she was about to remove. Superman watched with interest, his super-vision allowing him to see the individual wax molecules as they adhered to his skin.

"Ready?" the beautician asked, her hands poised to rip off the wax strip.

Superman nodded, his eyes flashing with a hint of amusement. "Bring it on," he said, trying to sound casual.

The beautician took a deep breath and yanked off the wax strip. Superman's eyes widened in surprise, and for a moment, he forgot to maintain his human guise. A faint "Up, up, and away!" escaped his lips as he instinctively flexed his super-strong muscles, ready to blast off into the stratosphere to escape the sudden pain.

But he quickly regained control, remembering that he was, after all, trying to be a normal human being. "Ow," he said, wincing in a very human-like way. "That did sting a bit."

The beautician smiled sympathetically. "I told you it might hurt," she said, applying a soothing lotion to Superman's newly waxed leg. "But you're doing great. Just a few more strips and we'll be done."
