(Sir Reginald, a medieval knight, sits at a wooden table, staring at a modern smartphone in confusion)

Sir Reginald: (to himself) What sorcery is this? A small glass window that showeth images and words, yet doth not respond to my touch.

(He pokes at the screen with his finger, trying to activate it)

Sir Reginald: (muttering) Fie upon it! Why doth it not obey my commands? Doth it require a password, like the secret phrase to enter the castle?

(He tries to type on the screen, but his armor-clad fingers are too bulky for the touchscreen)

Sir Reginald: (frustrated) Curses! My gauntlets are too large for this tiny keyboard. How doth one expect to type with such small keys?

(He tries to use the phone's voice assistant)

Sir Reginald: (into the phone) Hear me, good phone! I command thee to show me the way to the nearest tavern! And fetch me a pint of ale, forthwith!

(The phone responds with a robotic voice)

Phone: I'm sorry, I didn't quite catch that. Could you please repeat your request?

Sir Reginald: (confused) What manner of speech is this? Doth the phone not understand the King's English?

(He tries to use the phone's GPS)

Sir Reginald: (studying the screen) Ah, a map! Now, let us see... (he zooms in and out, trying to make sense of the digital landscape) Fie upon it! What sorcery is this? The roads and buildings seem to shift and change before mine eyes!

(He accidentally takes a selfie)

Sir Reginald: (startled) What in the name of all that is holy...? Why hath the phone captured my likeness? Is this some manner of dark magic?

(He tries to send a text message)

Sir Reginald: (dictating to the phone) Hear me, good phone! Send this message to my trusted squire, Sir Edward: "Meet me at the old oak tree at sundown. Come prepared for battle." (he looks at the screen) How doth one send this missive? Doth it require a carrier pigeon?

(He finally manages to send the message, but it's garbled and auto-corrected)
