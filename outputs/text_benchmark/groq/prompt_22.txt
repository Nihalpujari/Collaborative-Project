(Sir Reginald, a medieval knight, sits at a wooden table, staring at a modern smartphone in confusion)

Sir Reginald: (to himself) What sorcery is this? A small glass window that showeth images and words, yet doth not seem to be enchanted.

(He tentatively touches the screen with his armored finger, and the phone comes to life)

Sir Reginald: (startled) By the Lord above! What magic hath awakened this device?

(He begins to swipe through the screens, unsure of what he's doing)

Sir Reginald: (muttering) A calendar? What use hath a knight for a calendar? Doth it tell me when the next tournament shall be held?

(He opens the phone's web browser and is immediately overwhelmed by the vast amount of information)

Sir Reginald: (bewildered) So many words and images! How doth one make sense of it all? Is this some sort of magical tome?

(He accidentally takes a selfie, and his face appears on the screen)

Sir Reginald: (shouting) Ah! A mirror! But how doth it fit within this small glass window?

(He tries to make a call, but ends up accidentally dialing a random number)

Sir Reginald: (into the phone) Hello? Hello? Is this the castle's switchboard? I seek to speak with the king's messenger.

(A automated voice responds on the other end of the line)

Automated Voice: Welcome to Verizon. Your account balance is...

Sir Reginald: (confused) What sorcery is this? A voice from the heavens, speaking of... of... (he struggles to understand the automated voice) ...account balances?

(He hangs up the phone and throws it down on the table in frustration)

Sir Reginald: (to himself) This "smartphone" is a cursed device, sent to confound and bewilder
