The mummy, wrapped from head to toe in tattered, yellowed bandages, approached the revolving door with a slow, stiff gait. Its eyes, glowing with an otherworldly energy, fixed on the door as it pushed forward with a bony hand.

As the mummy entered the door, its bandages began to unravel, getting tangled in the door's metal frame. The mummy tried to push through, but its wrappings caught on the door's bars, causing it to become stuck.

The door, still revolving, began to wrap the mummy's bandages around its central axis, trapping the creature inside. The mummy let out a loud, mournful groan as it realized its predicament.

With each rotation, the mummy's bandages became more and more entangled, until it was completely immobilized. The door, now jammed, stopped revolving, leaving the mummy suspended in mid-air, its bandages resembling a giant, messy spider web.

People passing by the door couldn't help but stare at the absurd sight, some of them giggling at the mummy's misfortune. One brave soul, a maintenance worker, approached the door and tried to free the mummy, but it was a tricky task. The bandages were too tightly wrapped, and the mummy's dry, papery skin was starting to tear.

As the worker struggled to untangle the mummy, it let out a series of plaintive moans, its eyes glowing with an increasing intensity. Finally, after several minutes of wrestling with the bandages, the worker managed to extricate the mummy from the door.

The mummy stumbled out, its bandages still disheveled, and its dignity bruised. It glared at the door, then at the people watching, before slowly, stiffly, making its way away from the scene, muttering ancient curses under its breath. The onlookers, still chuckling, couldn't help but feel a little sorry for the hapless mummy, stuck in a revolving door, of all things.
