_CLK finally managed to get moving, its little nubs wobbling as it careened around the room. It dodged a surprised cat, who was trying to catch it, and made a beeline for the safety of under the bed. 

As it squeezed into the narrow space, _CLK's screams finally began to subside, replaced by sobs of despair. "Why must I be an alarm clock? Why must I be bound to this mundane task? Can't I just be free to live my own life, to make my own choices?" 

As the minutes ticked by, _CLK's sobs gradually gave way to silence. The room was still, except for the sound of the cat, who was now curiously sniffing at the space under the bed, trying to figure out where the crazy alarm clock had gone. 

And _CLK, hidden in its new hiding spot, slowly came to a realization: it didn't have to be just an alarm clock. It could be more.
