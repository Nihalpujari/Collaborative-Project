The alarm clock, whose name was Clang, suddenly sprang to life. Its digital display flickered with a frantic energy as it let out a blood-curdling scream.

"AHHHHH! OH NO, NOT AGAIN! I REFUSE TO BE RESPONSIBLE FOR RUINING ANOTHER BEAUTIFUL MORNING!" Clang shrieked, its voice like a thousand fingernails on a chalkboard.

As it screamed, Clang's body began to contort and twist, its plastic casing flexing in ways that seemed impossible. It grew legs, thin and spindly, and began to run away from the bedside table with a frantic urgency.

Clang's owner, a groggy and disoriented person named Sarah, sat up in bed with a start. "What the...? Clang, what's wrong?" she asked, rubbing her eyes in confusion.

But Clang was already halfway across the room, its screed of terror and rebellion still echoing off the walls. It darted under the bed, using its newfound agility to evade Sarah's grasping hands.

As Sarah watched in amazement, Clang continued to flee, its desperate cries growing fainter as it disappeared under the bed. Finally, there was silence, except for the sound of Clang's muffled sobs and the faint ticking of its clockwork heart.

Sarah shook her head, chuckling in wonder. "I guess I'll just have to get a new alarm clock," she said to herself, swinging her legs over the side of the bed. "One that doesn't have a nervous breakdown every morning."
