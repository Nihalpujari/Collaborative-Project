**Spider-Man on a Park Bench**
The late afternoon sun filters through the leaves of Central Park’s towering oaks, casting dappled shadows across the worn wooden slats of the bench. Spider-Man slouches there, his mask pulled up just enough to reveal his chin as he peels a slightly overripe banana with exaggerated care. The yellow peel curls back in a lazy spiral, and he takes a bite, chewing thoughtfully—*crunch*—before pausing to flick a stray fruit fly away with his free hand.

His suit is rumpled, the iconic red-and-blue fabric slightly scuffed at the knees (from that *one* rooftop chase where he misjudged the distance). A single web-line dangles from his wrist, swaying in the breeze like a forgotten thread. His boots rest on the bench’s edge, heels propped up, the soles worn thin from miles of swinging and landing. A half-empty bottle of orange Gatorade sits beside him, condensation beading on the plastic.

Across the path, a squirrel eyes him warily, clutching an acorn like it’s the last lifeboat on the *Titanic*. A kid on a scooter slows down, points, and whispers to their mom: *"Is that…?"* before being tugged away. Spider-Man waves absently, banana peel still in hand, then sighs and mutters to himself: *"Man, I *hate* when they’re this ripe."*

A pigeon lands nearby. He side-eyes it. *"Don’t even think about it."*

- **Pose:** Relaxed slouch, one leg bent, the other stretched out. The banana peel could be mid-drop or wrapped around his fingers.
