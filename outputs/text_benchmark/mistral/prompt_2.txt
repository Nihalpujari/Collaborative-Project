**The Red Fox at the Wheel**

A russet-furred red fox sits poised at a pottery wheel, its bushy tail curled neatly around its haunches like a living cushion. Its slender paws—delicate yet dexterous—press gently into the spinning clay, guiding the soft, wet earth into a graceful, swelling vase. The fox’s ears twitch with focus, one slightly bent forward as if listening to the hum of the wheel, while its golden eyes gleam with quiet concentration.

The clay, a rich terracotta, clings to its fur in places, leaving smudges on its muzzle and the tips of its toes. A few stray droplets of water glisten on its whiskers, evidence of the careful dips into the nearby bucket. The wheel spins steadily, its rhythmic whir blending with the soft *squelch* of the clay yielding to the fox’s touch.

Around the workbench, half-finished pots and bowls stand drying on wooden shelves, each bearing the faint imprint of the fox’s paw pads—tiny, accidental signatures of its craft. A coil of excess clay lies discarded like a sleeping serpent, waiting to be reshaped. The air smells of damp earth and the faint, musky scent of the fox itself, mingling with the warm, woody aroma of the studio.

In the background, a window lets in golden afternoon light, casting long shadows and painting the fox’s fur in fiery hues. Outside, the distant rustle of leaves and the occasional chirp of birds remind the artist that the world beyond the wheel is just as alive as the clay in its paws.
