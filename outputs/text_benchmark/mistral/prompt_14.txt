**Title: *Ripple & Roll***

A tiny, lime-green frog with bulging golden eyes perches on a lily pad, its webbed fingers dancing over the frets of a miniature electric guitar. The guitar—glossy black with neon-blue strings—is almost as big as the frog itself, its tiny amp (a repurposed acorn cap) humming with distortion. The frog’s tongue flicks out in concentration as it shreds a power chord, sending ripples across the pond’s surface.

Around it, the water shimmers with reflected stage lights (fireflies trapped in glass jars bobbing on nearby pads). A crowd of curious dragonflies hovers mid-air, their wings buzzing in time with the beat, while a sleepy turtle on a rock nods along, tapping its claw like a metronome. In the distance, a heron pauses mid-hunt, tilting its head—*is that… a frog playing blues riffs?*

The lily pad dips slightly under the weight of the performance, but the frog doesn’t notice. It’s lost in the music, its tiny chest puffed out like a rockstar, the last rays of sunset glinting off its slick skin. The pond is its arena. The guitar is its voice. And the ripples? Just the world’s first *frog-wave* encore.
