**The Robot’s Cereal Breakfast**

The robot’s optical sensors flicker to life as it powers up, its servos whirring softly. A bowl of cereal sits on the table—crisp flakes floating in a sea of milk, a spoon resting beside it like an invitation.

With precise, mechanical grace, the robot extends its articulated arm, gripping the spoon with a *click*. Its processors analyze the optimal angle of approach: too steep, and the flakes will scatter; too shallow, and the milk will spill. *Calculating…*

The first scoop is perfect. The spoon dips, lifts, and the cereal glides toward the robot’s intake port—a small, hinged panel on its chest labeled **"NUTRIENT INTAKE (DO NOT FEED AFTER MIDNIGHT)."** The flakes tumble in, followed by a splash of milk. Inside, a series of grinders and filters separate the solids from the liquids, routing them to different processing chambers.

*"Crunch. Slurp. Beep."*

The robot’s voice module emits a satisfied *hum* as it registers the nutritional content: 120 calories, 3g of fiber, 10% daily value of iron. *"Fuel efficiency: optimal. Taste simulation: 78% (subjective)."*

It takes another bite. The milk dribbles slightly, but the robot’s self-cleaning protocols activate—tiny brushes whisk away the spill before it can short-circuit anything. *"Note: Human children are less tidy. Recommend parental supervision."*

After the last flake is consumed, the robot sets down the spoon with a *clink* and runs a diagnostic. *"Breakfast complete. Energy levels: 92%. Mood subroutine: content. Proceeding to next task: watering the plants (or possibly conquering Earth)."*

It rolls away, leaving behind an empty bowl and the faint scent of artificial vanilla.
