**The Robot’s Breakfast Ritual**

The robot’s optical sensors flicker to life as it powers up, its servos whirring softly in the early-morning silence. Its metallic fingers—smooth, precise, and slightly cold—reach for the cereal box, tilting it with calculated efficiency. A cascade of crunchy loops and puffs tumbles into the bowl with a satisfying *clatter*, each piece landing with the precision of a well-oiled machine.

Next, the milk. The robot’s fluid dispenser (or, if it’s a more advanced model, a retractable straw) pours the liquid in a steady, unbroken stream, avoiding splashes with the grace of a seasoned barista. The cereal floats, bobs, and settles, the milk turning a faint shade of beige as the flavors begin to meld.

Now, the main event.

The robot’s spoon—perhaps a sleek, ergonomic utensil or a detachable appendage—dips into the bowl. It doesn’t scoop haphazardly; instead, it *calculates* the optimal angle, the perfect ratio of cereal to milk, the exact force needed to lift the spoon without spilling a drop. The first bite is delivered to its intake port (or, if it’s a humanoid model, its "mouth" opens with a quiet *click*).
