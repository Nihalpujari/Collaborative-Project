And there, in the center of it all, sat *him*: a single, slightly wilted hotdog, nestled in a porcelain dish meant for filet mignon.

His bun was a little too pale, his mustard a sad, congealed smear—like a tear that had given up halfway down his face. The restaurant hummed with the clink of wine glasses and the murmur of diners who had come for *ambiance*, for *experience*, for the kind of meal that required a sommelier’s blessing. A waiter in a crisp black vest passed by, his nose wrinkling slightly at the sight. *"Monsieur… is everything to your liking?"* he asked, though his tone suggested he already knew the answer.
