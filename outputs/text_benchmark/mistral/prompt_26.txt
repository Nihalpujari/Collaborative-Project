**Title: *The Mummy’s Spin Cycle***

The revolving door at the grand entrance of the *Museum of Ancient Oddities* spun smoothly—until it didn’t. With a sudden *thunk*, the mechanism jammed, trapping a very confused, very bandaged figure in its glass-and-brass prison.

The mummy, mid-stride after a long night of haunting the gift shop (mostly for the free mints), had misjudged the door’s rhythm. One moment, it was gliding forward with the dignity of a 3,000-year-old undead aristocrat; the next, its linen-wrapped arms were splayed against the glass like a starfish on a windshield. The door, now stuck in a perpetual 45-degree limbo, refused to budge.

"Unhand me, you *modern contraption*!" the mummy hissed (or at least, that’s what the hieroglyphs on its bandages *probably* translated to). It wriggled, tugged, and even attempted a dramatic curse—only for the door to creak ominously in response.

A security guard, sipping coffee nearby, nearly dropped his cup. "Sir, are you… *stuck*?"

The mummy’s bandaged head swiveled toward him. "*Sir*? I am *Pharaoh Khufu the Unyielding*! And yes, I am *trapped* in your *infernal spinning box*!"

The guard, now sweating, radioed for backup. "Uh, we’ve got a… uh… *historical artifact* in the revolving door. Send the curator. And maybe an exorcist."
