**The Mummy’s Revolving Door Fiasco**

The ancient, linen-wrapped mummy shuffled forward, its bandages whispering against the marble floor of the grand museum lobby. It had spent centuries in a sarcophagus, dreaming of the outside world—only to wake up in the *worst* possible place: a modern building with *revolving doors*.

The first panel of the door swung open with a *whoosh*. The mummy lurched inside, arms outstretched like a sleepwalker. But then—**disaster**. Its brittle, centuries-old wrappings snagged on the metal frame. The door kept turning. The mummy *did not*.

**"UNNNNNNNNGH—"** it groaned (or at least, that’s what mummies sound like in cartoons) as its bandages unraveled like a cheap sweater caught in a fan. One arm flailed free, then a leg, then—**SNAP**—a strip of linen tore loose, revealing a dusty, skeletal finger pointing accusingly at the door’s mechanism.

The revolving door, now jammed with a half-unwrapped mummy, ground to a halt. Tourists screamed. A security guard fumbled for his walkie-talkie. **"Uh… we’ve got a Code… *Bandage* in the lobby."**

Meanwhile, the mummy’s remaining wrappings fluttered in the breeze from the door’s motion, like a ghostly flag of surrender. Its hollow eye sockets stared at the ceiling in eternal embarrassment.

**Moral of the story?** *Never trust a door that spins.*
