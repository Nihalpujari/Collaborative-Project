**A Cloud‑Gum Dream**

*In the high blue, a fluffy white cloud drifted lazily across the summer sky. But this was no ordinary vapor‑mass. It had a secret, mischievous habit—every now and then it would scoop up a pinch of sugary, pink‑pink bubble‑gum from the invisible pantry of the atmosphere and begin to blow.*

*Its soft, cottony lips puckered, and a thin ribbon of pink gum stretched out from its underside, spiraling into the air. The gum glistened in the sunlight, catching tiny rainbows as it caught the sun’s rays. With a gentle exhale, the cloud puffed out a gigantic, translucent bubble that floated up, swaying like a lazy kite. Inside the bubble, tiny vignettes flickered—miniature sun‑lit fields, a flock of sparrows, a distant mountain—each scene fading in and out as the bubble drifted higher.*

*The bubble grew, shimmering, its surface rippling with the faint wind. Children on a hill below pointed upward, mouths forming perfect “O” shapes, imitating the cloud’s playful act. The cloud giggled—if clouds could giggle—sending a soft drizzle of sweet‑scented mist that smelled faintly of strawberry licorice. When the bubble finally popped, it released a spray of pink‑pink foam that fell like confetti, kissing rooftops and grass blades with a fleeting, sugary kiss before vanishing.*
