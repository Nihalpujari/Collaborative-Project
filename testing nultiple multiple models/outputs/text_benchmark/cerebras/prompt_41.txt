**A Couch Sitting on a Human Living‑Room Floor – A Mini‑Scene**

The room breathes easy, its walls painted a soft, cloud‑gray that catches the late‑afternoon light filtering through sheer curtains. The floor is a warm, honey‑toned hardwood, its grain revealing faint whispers of age and countless footfalls. Directly upon this board‑plank stage sits a couch—an anachronism, a piece of furniture that has chosen to forgo its usual legs and sit squarely on the floor as if it were a giant, cushioned rug.

* **Shape & Silhouette**  
  The couch is a low‑profile, mid‑century modern piece: clean, angular lines softened by generous, rounded armrests. Its backrest, barely a few inches above the seat, leans forward in a relaxed, almost reclined posture. The absence of legs gives it a solid, monolithic presence, as if the sofa itself were a continuation of the floor—its base flush and seamless with the hardwood beneath.
