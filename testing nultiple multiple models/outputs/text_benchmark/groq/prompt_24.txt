(The scene is set in a bustling salon, with the sound of hair dryers and chatter filling the air. Superman, dressed in a simple white t-shirt and shorts, lies on a waxing table, looking less than comfortable. A aesthetician, a no-nonsense woman with a kind smile, stands over him, holding a wax strip.)

Aesthetician: Okay, Superman, this might sting a bit. Are you ready?

Superman: (gritting his teeth) I've taken on Darkseid, Brainiac, and Doomsday. I think I can handle a little wax.

Aesthetician: (smirking) We'll see about that. (she applies the wax to his leg and rips off the strip)

Superman: (wincing) Ah! That's... quite a sensation.

Aesthetician: (laughing) I told you it might hurt. You're not as invulnerable as you think you are, are you?

Superman: (sheepishly) I guess not when it comes to waxing. (he looks down at his leg, where a red patch is starting to form)

Aesthetician: (smiling) Don't worry, it's just temporary. You'll be back to saving the world in no time. (she applies another wax strip)

Superman: (taking a deep breath) Okay, bring it on. (the aesthetician rips off the strip, and Superman lets out a loud "Ow!")

Aesthetician: (laughing) You're doing great, Superman! Almost done.

Superman: (panting) I... I think I need a minute. (he looks up at the ceiling, trying to compose himself)

Aesthetician: (smiling) Take your time, Superman. I'll just get some more wax. (she walks away, leaving Superman to collect himself)

(As she returns with more wax, Superman looks up at her with a determined glint in his eye.)

Superman: (resolutely) Okay, let's finish this. I'm not going to let a little wax defeat me.

Aesthetician: (grinning) That's the spirit! (she applies the wax and rips off the strip, Superman wincing in response)

Superman: (exhaling) That's... it. I think I'm done.

Aesthetician: (smiling) You did great, Superman!
