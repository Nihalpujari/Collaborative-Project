The squirrel, whose name was Nutmeg, stood on his hind legs, his paws grasping the tiny gold smartphone as he barked orders into the receiver. His business suit, a miniature version of those worn by human executives, was immaculately tailored to fit his slender squirrel frame. A crisp white shirt and a patterned tie added to his professional demeanor, but it was his fierce expression that commanded attention.

"What do you mean the acorn shipment is delayed?" Nutmeg demanded, his voice rising in indignation. "I specifically instructed you to have them delivered by dawn today. Do you know how much this is going to cost me? I have a meeting with the Chipmunk Investment Group in an hour, and I need those acorns to close the deal."

As he spoke, Nutmeg paced back and forth across the branch of a tall oak tree, his tail twitching with agitation. His beady eyes gleamed with a fierce intensity, and his sharp teeth were bared in a snarl.

"I don't pay you to make excuses, I pay you to get results," Nutmeg snapped, his voice dripping with disdain. "Get the acorns to me within the hour, or you're fired. Do I make myself clear?"

There was a pause as the person on the other end of the line responded, and Nutmeg's expression turned from anger to calculation.

"Fine," he said finally, his voice dripping with sarcasm. "I'll give you an extra 15 minutes. But if those acorns aren't here by then, you'll be sorry you ever crossed paths with Nutmeg, CEO of Squirrelington Industries."

With a final glare, Nutmeg snapped the phone shut and tucked it into his breast pocket. He smoothed his tie and adjusted his suit jacket, his composure restored. He was a squirrel on a mission, and nothing was going to stand in his way.
